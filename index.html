Program Overview & Disclaimer
Everything you need to know before your first session.

Training Level

This is a 3 day per week program. Complete each workout (Fullbody A, Fullbody B, and Fullbody C) once per week. I recommend having at least one rest day in between each workout. I also recommend a general warm up before each workout to activate key muscles, improve movement quality, and reduce risk of injury. For an optimal warm up, I recommend 10 - 15 minutes of light cardio paired with stretching and/or foam rolling muscles that are tight.

JakeFitness Tip

Most exercises can be done on machines or with free weights. I've listed my recommendations, but try different variations and stick with what feels most comfortable and engaging for your body.

Chapter 02
Training Guidelines
The principles that drive every rep, set, and session.
  • 1
    Start light. When starting a new exercise, always start with a lighter weight to get a feel for the range of motion and muscle contraction. I recommend lifting with a slow and controlled pace - focusing on maintaining good form, full range of motion, and mind muscle connection.
  • 2
    Rep ranges should be a real challenge. With the example rep range of 10 - 15, the 10th to 15th rep should be challenging to complete while still maintaining good form. If you can't lift the weight for at least 10 reps with good form then decrease the weight, it’s too heavy. On the reverse, if you can lift the weight for 15+ reps without pushing yourself or trying too hard then you need to increase the weight. Apply this logic to all rep ranges.
  • 3
    Progressive overload every session. Let’s say you squat 135lbs for 10 reps. The next workout that you do squats, push yourself to squat 135lbs for 11 reps. If you get that extra rep, great! Then next time you squat, push yourself to squat 135lbs for 12 reps. If you couldn't lift it for 11 reps then try for 11 reps again the next workout. Increase reps first, before increasing the amount of weight. Quality movement always comes before adding weight. Apply this across all exercises. Your reps WILL NOT increase EVERY workout but if you keep pushing yourself and consume adequate nutrition, your strength and muscle mass WILL increase over time.
  • 4
    Rest 1 - 2 minutes between sets. Adjust based on how you're recovering. Rest a little longer for heavier compound lifts, rest a little less for isolation work.
  • 5
    Track everything. I HIGHLY RECOMMEND logging your workouts (write down the exercises, amount of weight lifted, and sets/reps completed). I also highly recommend logging your nutrition (calories & protein), taking weekly progression pictures, tracking weekly bodyweight, and monthly body part measurements in order to track progress.
  • 6
    To increase intensity: add reps, weight, or sets over time. You can also train closer to failure, shorten rest times, or incorporate pause reps, supersets, and drop sets. Adjust your volume and intensity (number of sets, amount of weight, rep ranges, rest time) according to your muscle soreness and recovery time. Increase volume and intensity as your body allows, listen to your body and learn your limits.

Exercises
Goblet Squat
Builds leg and glute strength while improving squat mechanics
2 sets8–12 reps
Instructions
  • Hold dumbbell/kettlebell vertically against chest
  • Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out
  • Hinge at hips, bend knees, chest tall
  • Lower until thighs parallel — press through heels
Form Cues
  • Push knees out, tracking over toes
  • Keep heels planted throughout
  • Neutral spine, chest upright
  • Inhale down, exhale up
Avoid
  • Knees caving inward
  • Rounding chest or lower back
  • Heels lifting off the ground
Chest Press Machine
Strengthens chest, shoulders, and triceps
3 sets8–12 reps
Instructions
  • Sit with back fully supported, feet flat
  • Adjust handles to chest height
  • Press forward until elbows almost straight
  • Slowly return to starting position
Form Cues
  • Elbows at ~30–45° from torso
  • Shoulder blades down and back
  • Control the lowering phase
Avoid
  • Flaring elbows too wide
  • Using momentum to push
  • Excessive lower back arch
Watch
V-Squat Machine
Strengthens quads, hamstrings, and glutes with guided movement
3 sets10–15 reps
Instructions
  • Shoulders under pads, feet shoulder-width
  • Brace core, unlock safety handle
  • Lower until thighs parallel — drive through heels
  • Re-engage safety after set
Form Cues
  • Chest up, back against pad at all times
  • Knees track in line with toes
  • Push through heels and mid-foot
Avoid
  • Knees caving inward
  • Back lifting off the pad
  • Heels lifting or bouncing at bottom

Dumbbell Shoulder Press
Strengthens the shoulders while improving stability
2 sets10–15 reps
Instructions
  • Sit on bench with back support, feet flat
  • Dumbbells at shoulder height, palms forward
  • Press overhead until arms almost straight
  • Slowly return to shoulder height
Form Cues
  • Core engaged to protect lower back
  • Wrists stacked over elbows throughout
  • Shoulder blades down and back
Avoid
  • Excessive lower back arch
  • Elbows flaring too wide
  • Using momentum or bouncing
Watch
Tricep Cable Pushdown
Isolates and strengthens the triceps
2 sets10–15 reps
Instructions
  • Rope/bar on high pulley, stand facing machine
  • Elbows tucked at sides at ~90°
  • Push to full extension — slowly return
Form Cues
  • Elbows fixed at sides throughout
  • Chest up, shoulders down
  • Only the elbows move
Avoid
  • Elbows flaring or drifting forward
  • Leaning body weight into bar
  • Letting weight slam back up

Exercises
Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift
Strengthens hamstrings, glutes, and lower back
3 sets8–12 reps
Instructions
  • Dumbbells in front of thighs, feet hip-width
  • Hinge hips back, lower along shins
  • Lower until hamstring stretch, mid-shin
  • Drive hips forward to standing
Form Cues
  • Neutral spine — no rounding lower back
  • Shoulders pulled back, chest lifted
  • Slight knee bend throughout
Avoid
  • Bending knees too much (becomes a squat)
  • Rounding the lower back
  • Dumbbells drifting away from shins

Seated Cable Row
Strengthens mid-back and biceps while improving pulling strength
3 sets8–12 reps
Instructions
  • Feet on platform, knees slightly bent
  • Arms fully extended, sit tall
  • Pull handle to bellybutton, drive elbows back
  • Squeeze shoulder blades — slowly extend
Form Cues
  • Neutral spine throughout
  • Lead with elbows, not hands
  • Shoulders down and back
Avoid
  • Swinging too far back
  • Rounding shoulders forward at start
  • Pulling bar too high toward chest
Watch
Smith Machine Hip Thrust
Builds glutes and improves hip extension strength
3 sets8–12 reps
Instructions
  • Upper back on bench, bar over hips (use pad)
  • Feet flat, hip-width apart
  • Drive through heels — thrust hips up parallel
  • Squeeze glutes at top, lower slowly
Form Cues
  • Chin tucked, ribs down — neutral spine
  • Shins nearly vertical at top
  • Push through heels, not toes
Avoid
  • Overextending the lower back at top
  • Feet too far or too close
  • Knees caving inward
Watch
Lat Pulldown Machine
Strengthens lats and biceps
3 sets10–15 reps
Instructions
  • Thighs under pads, feet flat on floor
  • Grip slightly wider than shoulder-width
  • Lean back ~10–15°, chest up
  • Pull bar to upper chest — slowly release
Form Cues
  • Drive elbows down and back
  • Chest lifted, shoulder blades engaged
  • No swinging or momentum
Avoid
  • Pulling bar behind neck
  • Leaning too far back
  • Using momentum over muscle

Incline Dumbbell Bicep Curl
Isolates and strengthens the biceps
2 sets10–15 reps
Instructions
  • Bench at 30–45°, head and shoulders supported
  • Arms at full extension, palms forward
  • Curl toward shoulders — squeeze at top
  • Slowly lower back to start
Form Cues
  • Shoulders back against the bench
  • Elbows stay slightly behind torso
  • Move only at the elbow
Avoid
  • Swinging or using momentum
  • Elbows drifting forward during curl
  • Shortening range of motion at bottom

Exercises
Leg Press (High Feet Placement)
Strengthens quads, hamstrings, and glutes with back support
3 sets8–12 reps
Instructions
  • Sit fully supported, head and back against pad
  • Feet shoulder-width on upper platform, toes out
  • Release safety, lower to ~90° knee bend
  • Drive through heels — re-engage safety after
Form Cues
  • Lower back pressed into seat at all times
  • Knees track in line with toes
  • Feet flat throughout movement
Avoid
  • Locking knees out at top
  • Knees caving inward
  • Hips rolling off seat at bottom
Watch
Half Kneeling Landmine Press
Strengthens shoulders, triceps, and core stability
3 sets8–12 reps
Instructions
  • Barbell in landmine or corner
  • Kneel on one knee, other foot forward
  • Barbell at shoulder height — brace core
  • Press up and forward — slowly lower
Form Cues
  • Torso upright, core braced — resist rotation
  • Front knee over ankle for stability
  • Press through shoulder, not back
Avoid
  • Torso twisting or leaning
  • Elbows flaring during press
  • Poor foot placement compromising balance
Watch
Walking Lunges
Builds quads, glutes, hamstrings — improves balance and coordination
2 sets10–15 / leg
Instructions
  • Stand tall, feet hip-width (dumbbells optional)
  • Step forward — lower until both knees ~90°
  • Back knee hovers above floor
  • Drive through front heel into next lunge
Form Cues
  • Chest up, core engaged throughout
  • Front knee tracks in line with toes
  • Push through front heel to rise
Avoid
  • Front knee caving inward
  • Steps too short or too long
  • Pushing off primarily from back foot
Watch
Dumbbell Farmer's Carry
Builds grip, core stability, and full-body functional strength
3 sets30–60 sec
Instructions
  • Dumbbells at sides, stand tall, brace core
  • Walk forward with controlled, steady steps
  • Maintain upright posture throughout
  • Lower safely by hinging at hips when done
Form Cues
  • Chest up, shoulders down — no shrugging
  • Core tight, resist lateral lean
  • Short controlled steps, head neutral
Avoid
  • Leaning excessively to one side
  • Shrugging shoulders toward ears
  • Fast, uncontrolled steps
Watch
Plank with Shoulder Taps
Strengthens core and improves anti-rotation stability
2 sets10–20 / side
Instructions
  • High plank, hands under shoulders, feet hip-width
  • Engage core, squeeze glutes — straight body line
  • Lift one hand, tap opposite shoulder
  • Alternate sides, 1–2 sec per tap
Form Cues
  • Hips level — no rocking side to side
  • Core braced as if absorbing a hit
  • Press the floor away to stabilize shoulders
Avoid
  • Rocking hips during taps
  • Lower back sagging
  • Raising hips too high

Nutrition, Protein & Hydration
Fuel your training and maximize muscle growth.

Understanding the three macronutrients - protein, carbs, and fats - is the foundation of any effective diet. You can train perfectly, but without adequate calories and macros, muscle growth and goal progress will be severely limited.

The Three Macronutrients
Protein

Builds and repairs muscle tissue. Essential for growth and recovery.

Sources: meat, fish, eggs, dairy, beans.

Provides 4 calories per gram.

Carbohydrates

Your body's primary energy source - especially for intense training.

Sources: rice, oats, potatoes, fruit, veggies, bread.

Provides 4 calories per gram.

Fats

Supports hormone production, joint health, and vitamin absorption.

Sources: oils, nuts, avocado, fatty fish, eggs.

Provides 9 calories per gram.

Bulking vs. Cutting
Bulking

Bulking is gaining bodyweight (both muscle and fat) by being in a calorie surplus (consuming more calories than your body is burning). Best for ectomorph/mesomorph body types and those prioritizing strength gains.

Protein0.8 - 1.0g per lb bodyweight
Carbs40 - 50% of total calories
Fats25 - 35% of total calories
Cutting

Cutting is losing bodyweight (mostly fat while preserving muscle) by being in a calorie deficit (burning more calories than your are consuming). Best for endomorph body types and those wanting to lose fat.

Protein1.0 - 1.2g per lb bodyweight
Carbs30 - 40% of total calories
Fats20 - 30% of total calories

The Golden Rule

Aim for 1g of protein per 1lb of bodyweight daily - especially on training days. This is the single most important nutritional lever for building and preserving muscle.

Example: Someone who weighs 150 lbs should aim to consume around 150g of protein daily.

Hydration

As a baseline — take your bodyweight in lbs and divide by 2 — aim to drink this number in ounces of water per day. Increase this number on training days.

Example: Someone who weighs 150 lbs should aim to drink at least 75 oz of water daily.
